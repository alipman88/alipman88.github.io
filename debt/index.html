Elizabeth Warren's bill ties Stafford Stafford loan interest to the 0.75 percent rate paid by big banks. The current rate is 3.4 percent; this is set to double on July 1 if Republican legislators prevent congress from passing student loan reform. The house GOP plan uses a fluctuating interest rate tied to the 10-year treasury yield, with an 8.5% cap. This means a student could take out a loan at a reasonable rate, but find themselves paying 8.5% a few years down the line, essentially a bait-and-switch.
